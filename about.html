From Spreadsheets to Systems

15 years in the trenches of accounting, research finance, federal compliance, and nonprofit operations. Now I build the automation I wish I'd had.

My Story


I've spent my career in the weeds of financial operations at organizations that do important work — healthcare research at Providence Health & Services and OHSU, regional governance at Metro, and public accounting at Deloitte.

I've built indirect cost rate proposals. I've survived single audits. I've reconciled sub-award budgets against general ledger activity. I've sat in rooms explaining 2 CFR 200 compliance to program directors who just wanted to do their research.

Every one of those processes was manual. Repetitive. And ripe for automation.

When AI tools matured enough to actually handle the complexity of regulated financial operations, I made a shift. I stopped being the person doing the work manually and started building systems that do it better, faster, and without the human error that keeps auditors employed.

I hold a CPA license (in Oregon) and an MBA. I've managed federal awards from NIH, NSF, HRSA, and SAMHSA. I understand 2 CFR 200 at the line-item level — not because I read about it, but because I lived it.

Now I bring that same depth to building AI automation that actually works in regulated environments. Not chatbots. Not generic workflow tools. Purpose-built automation for the specific pain points I spent 15 years experiencing firsthand.
